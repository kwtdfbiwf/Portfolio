Fixit — Repair App

Fixit is an app designed to make repairing everyday items feel achievable, even for people with zero technical experience. Instead of immediately replacing broken items, FixIt helps users decide whether something is worth fixing, guides them through the process step by step, and supports them with community and AI help along the way. The aim is to save money, reduce waste, and make fixing things feel less intimidating.

Overview

The Problem

Most people instinctively choose to replace broken items despite repair often being cheaper and more sustainable. This behaviour is drivren by a few key problems. These being: Perceive cost, lack of confidence and knowledge, convenience and replacement culture, and lack of clear support.

Understanding

Research & Discovery

To uncover why users avoid repair, research focused on user behaviour, motivations, and pain points. Surveys, interviews, and secondary research revealed that consumers value sustainability and cost savings but are blocked by low confidence, cognitive overload, and uncertainty. Competitor analysis highlighted gaps in current solutions, showing that most apps either overwhelm users or fail to clearly guide them through the repair process. This insight informed the design priorities for FixIt.

Process

Design & Finalising

The design process centred on simplifying repair and reducing perceived risk. Wireframes and prototypes were iteratively tested to ensure clarity, usability, and engagement. Features like step by step guides, cost comparison, checklists, AI support, and a community forum were developed in response to user needs. Iterative testing and feedback refined the interface and interactions, ensuring the app empowers users to repair with confidence while keeping the experience straightforward and motivating.

Results

Outcome

FixIt successfully addresses the main barriers to repair by combining guidance, reassurance, and community support. Users can make informed decisions with clear step by step instructions, cost comparisons, and checklists, reducing uncertainty and building confidence. The app encourages repair over replacement, helping users save money, extend the life of their possessions, and feel empowered in everyday problem-solving. Early testing and feedback indicate that users find the process approachable, motivating, and more likely to influence future repair behaviours.
