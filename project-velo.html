Overview

The Problem

TheCubicle.com's current website can feel overwhelming and cluttered. There are too many filters and tags, making it hard for users to find what they're looking for without getting lost in options. Visually, the site feels cheap and tacky, with inconsistent styling and crowded layouts that add to the confusion. All of these issues create a frustrating experience, especially for new or casual visitors, and make it difficult for the site to present itself as a trusted, professional destination for speedcubers.

Understanding

Design Principles

To fully understand the issues with TheCubicle.com, I conducted a heuristic evaluation and created a site flow map. The heuristic evaluation helped identify key usability problems, such as overwhelming navigation, confusing filters, and inconsistent visual design. Meanwhile, the site flow map clarified how users move through the website, highlighting bottlenecks, dead ends, and areas where too many options create friction. Together, these tools provided a clear picture of both the functional and visual challenges, guiding the redesign process toward a more intuitive, streamlined, and visually cohesive user experience.

Process

Design & Finalising

Using established design principles, I recreated TheCubicle.com in Figma with a focus on clarity and usability. The layout was reorganised to guide users naturally through the page, while unnecessary filters were removed and tags refined to only the most relevant options. The design features a cleaner, more cohesive colour scheme and simplified page structure, creating a visually appealing and intuitive experience. Overall, the redesign turns a cluttered, overwhelming site into a smooth, organised platform that's easy to navigate for cubers of all levels
